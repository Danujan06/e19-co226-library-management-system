Library
Management
System

A library management system (LMS) is a software application that helps libraries manage their collections, patrons, and circulation. LMSs typically include features for: Acquisitions: Tracking the purchase of new materials Cataloging: Describing and classifying materials Circulation: Tracking the lending and returning of materials Patron management: Tracking the registration and checkout history of patrons Reporting: Generating reports on library usage LMSs can be used by libraries of all sizes, from small public libraries to large academic libraries. They can help libraries to improve efficiency, save time, and provide better service to their patrons.
